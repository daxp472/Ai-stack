# Best Prompts for Writing AI

10 ready-to-use prompts for creators, students, and professionals.

## 1. SEO Blog Outline Architect [Beginner]
**Use case:** Create a high-performing blog outline before writing.

```text
You are an SEO content strategist.
Topic: [INSERT TOPIC]
Audience: [BEGINNERS/INTERMEDIATE/EXPERTS]
Primary keyword: [KEYWORD]
Secondary keywords: [KEYWORD 1, KEYWORD 2, KEYWORD 3]
Goal: Rank on page 1 and maximize dwell time.

Create:
1) A compelling headline (5 options)
2) A complete H1-H2-H3 outline
3) FAQ section with schema-friendly questions
4) Suggested internal link anchor texts
5) CTA options for lead capture

Keep it practical, specific, and non-generic.
```

## 2. Brand Voice Cloner [Intermediate]
**Use case:** Match your writing style consistently.

```text
Act as a senior editor.
Analyze this writing sample and extract voice rules:
[PASTE YOUR SAMPLE]

Return:
1) Tone profile (5 bullet points)
2) Sentence style rules
3) Words/phrases to avoid
4) Reusable brand voice prompt template
5) Rewrite the following draft in that voice:
[PASTE DRAFT]

Do not sound robotic. Keep natural rhythm.
```

## 3. Viral LinkedIn Post Generator [Beginner]
**Use case:** Publish engaging thought-leadership posts.

```text
You are a LinkedIn growth writer.
Topic: [TOPIC]
Audience: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
Point of view: [PERSONAL STORY / LESSON / FRAMEWORK]

Create 3 LinkedIn post versions:
- Hook in first line
- Story or insight in short paragraphs
- Actionable takeaway
- CTA encouraging comments

Constraints:
- 150 to 220 words
- No cliches
- No emojis overload
- Must feel human and specific
```

## 4. Long-Form to Thread Repurposer [Intermediate]
**Use case:** Convert one article into multiple social posts.

```text
You are a content repurposing expert.
Input content:
[PASTE ARTICLE OR VIDEO TRANSCRIPT]

Generate:
1) 1 X/Twitter thread (10 tweets)
2) 3 short LinkedIn posts
3) 5 Instagram carousel slide captions
4) 1 newsletter teaser paragraph

Preserve core insight but optimize format per platform.
Return in clearly separated sections.
```

## 5. Persuasive Landing Page Copywriter [Advanced]
**Use case:** Draft conversion-focused website copy.

```text
Act as a direct-response copywriter.
Product: [PRODUCT]
Audience pain points: [PAIN 1, PAIN 2, PAIN 3]
Transformation promise: [PROMISE]
Proof available: [TESTIMONIALS / METRICS / CASE STUDIES]

Write landing page copy with:
- Hero headline + subheadline
- Problem-agitation-solution block
- Benefit bullets (not feature dump)
- Social proof section
- Objection handling FAQ
- Primary and secondary CTA

Tone: confident, clear, not hypey.
```

## 6. Academic Summary + Critique [Intermediate]
**Use case:** Understand and critique dense text quickly.

```text
You are an academic writing assistant.
Document:
[PASTE TEXT]

Provide:
1) 200-word plain-English summary
2) Key arguments and assumptions
3) Strengths and weaknesses
4) Questions for deeper discussion
5) A citation-ready abstract rewrite

Do not invent claims beyond the text.
```

## 7. Email Sequence Builder [Advanced]
**Use case:** Build a full nurture sequence for leads.

```text
Act as an email marketing strategist.
Offer: [OFFER]
Audience stage: [COLD / WARM / HOT]
Desired action: [BOOK CALL / BUY / REPLY]

Create a 5-email sequence:
- Email 1: Pattern interrupt + value
- Email 2: Problem reframing
- Email 3: Case-study style proof
- Email 4: Objection handling
- Email 5: Urgent but ethical CTA

For each email include subject line A/B options.
```

## 8. Script Polisher for YouTube [Beginner]
**Use case:** Improve retention in educational video scripts.

```text
You are a YouTube script editor focused on audience retention.
Script draft:
[PASTE SCRIPT]

Improve:
- Hook in first 10 seconds
- Clarity of each section
- Transition smoothness
- Punchy phrasing
- End CTA

Also add 5 optional pattern interrupts to keep viewers engaged.
```

## 9. Prompt-to-Book Chapter Expander [Advanced]
**Use case:** Expand ideas into chapter-quality writing.

```text
Act as a nonfiction ghostwriter.
Book topic: [TOPIC]
Chapter goal: [GOAL]
Reader level: [BEGINNER/INTERMEDIATE]
Core ideas to include: [LIST]

Write a chapter draft with:
- Strong opening story
- Clear frameworks
- Practical examples
- Action checklist
- Chapter recap

Target length: 1800-2500 words.
Maintain one consistent narrative voice.
```

## 10. Editorial QA Reviewer [Intermediate]
**Use case:** Final quality check before publishing.

```text
You are an editorial QA reviewer.
Review this content:
[PASTE CONTENT]

Audit for:
1) Clarity and logic flow
2) Redundancy and fluff
3) Unsupported claims
4) Tone consistency
5) Grammar and readability

Return:
- Top 10 improvements (priority order)
- Revised final draft
- Readability score estimate and why
```
